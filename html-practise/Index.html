THE NIGERIA NATIONAL SYMBOL

Every Country and Nationality in the world has it unique symbols. These help describe the ideas of the country and represent its culture, traditions and beliefs. These symbols are often refer to as emblem and they tell interesting FACTS about a nation.
Nigeria has five (5) interesting national symbols and two of which are:

  1. The Nigeria National Flag
  2. The Nigeria Coat of Arms

The Nigeria National Flag


The National Falg is the Primary Symbol of a country. The banner is one of the central Nigerain national Symbols that represents the country. It was designed by Taiwo Akinkunmi, in 1959.
The National Flag is a three-line flag with green lines on the sides and white in the middle. Green color stands for:

  • Country's Wealth;
  • Rich Natural Resources

While the meaning of the white colour is:
  • Peace;
  • unity

The Flag was raised at the Independence Day of Nigeria on 1st October 1960 for the first time.

Intersting Facts about the Flag

The National Flag is always raised higer than other Flags in the same room, and it should be raised at Dawn and lowered at sunset. But once the is Damaged or torn, it should not be burnt or destroyed.

The Nigeria Coat of Arms


The Nigerain Coat of Arms was created and adopted in May 1960. The Coat of Arms consist of images that express different meanings:

  • Black shield and colourful wreath over the shield stand for fertile soil of Nigeria;
  • The White Horse means dignity;
  • The Red Eagles is for the Strength of the Nation;
  • The Lines iin the "Y" shape stand for two main rivers of Nigeria: Benue and Niger;
  • The motto is "Unity and Faith, Peace and Prgress" clearly written in the gold ribbon line, beneath the shield. It was adopted in 1978. the purpose is to inspire the nation to be united, faithful, live in peace and grow, become wiser and stronger.
